Island Hopping in Thailand!

By Kiersten

Most people who arrive into Thailand will connect through Bangkok and will start their hopping loop in Phuket, like I did. There are several different ways to get from Bangkok to Phuket, and while bus options are usually the cheapest, costing the equivalent of around $20, they also can take up to thirteen hours, and often will be in fairly cramped conditions.

Traveling by train and then a shorter bus will usually be around six hours, and cost the equivalent of around $30, while a flight is the quickest way, and usually costs around the equivalent of $50 if booked in advance.

It is important to be careful when you are island hopping, as the ferries between each island may be inexpensive, but they don't always have the best safety record. Ensure that you have a look at the ferry to check there are sufficient lifeboats and that it isn't too crowded before boarding, and if you have any doubts, it may be best to go with another ferry company.

Thai people are generally very friendly, hence the nickname "The Land of Smiles," but always use your best judgement. Also, Thai drug laws are also incredibly strict, so don't take any risks.
