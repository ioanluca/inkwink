Adventure In Chinese Cuisine

By Paul Bernhardt

The September opening of Mike Chang's Chinese Restaurant marked the culmination of a longtime dream and the beginning of a new adventure. Chang, who has been maitre d' at two of the region's best Chinese restaurants - Uncle Lui's Peking Mandarin and Chef Wang's Szechuan Wok, both in Cherry Hill - longed to have his own restaurant.

He got his chance in the summer and took over the building on Route 73 in Maple Shade that had housed the Panda restaurant. Chang renovated it in Western-style decor, brought in an accomplished chef from New York and ended up with a small, attractive place serving good Chinese cuisine. The chef uses fresh ingredients, and portions are generous. Although I was recognized, all other patrons seemed to receive the same food and service. Still, selections had to be made with discrimination, for not every dish was made in heaven.

The attractive pink-and-gray dining room has gray wallpaper with pink chrysanthemums, gray booths with white tablecloths, bouquets of fresh flowers and candles in glass lamps with shades. Western-style Chinese love songs recorded by Theresa Tang, the most popular Asian singer of the day, play softly in the background.

The restaurant has no liquor license, but patrons are welcome to bring their own beverages. Service from an English-speaking waiter was excellent.
